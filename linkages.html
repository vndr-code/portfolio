Linkages and Gears – Kinetic Mechanisms

This section explores kinetic structures driven by custom-designed linkages and gears, showcasing how mechanical systems can translate rotational motion into complex and dynamic movements.

Designing Custom Gears

The general gear types:

  • Spur Gears: The most common gear type used, featuring straight teeth parallel to the gear’s axis, ideal for transferring motion between parallel shafts.
  • Bevel Gears: Designed to transfer motion between intersecting shafts, usually at a 90-degree angle, allowing for directional changes in motion.
  • Worm Gears: A gear configuration that provides high torque and reduces speed, useful in situations requiring controlled, gradual movements.
  • Planetary Gears: A system that combines multiple gears to create compact, high-torque mechanisms, often used in complex movement sequences.

Linkage Mechanisms

Linkages are pivotal in translating rotary motion from gears into linear or complex motions:

  • Four-Bar Linkages: Simple yet effective configurations used to generate continuous looping or oscillating motions.
  • Legged Linkages: Inspired by walking mechanisms, these linkages are designed with precise lengths and pivot points to simulate natural gaits.
  • Harmonic Linkages: Used in the kinetic tower project, these linkages transform uniform gear rotations into flowing, wave-like facade movements.

Project Highlights

  • Legged Linkage Mechanism: A multi-legged kinetic structure where gears coordinate the timing of leg movements, ensuring alternating strides for stability and natural motion.
  • Kinetic Tower with Dynamic Facade: A tower structure featuring interconnected linkages and gears that transform the facade’s shape. As gears rotate, the facade dynamically morphs through coordinated linkage-driven movements.

Implementation and Fabrication

All components were designed using CAD software, focusing on gear ratios, linkage lengths, and pivot placements to ensure smooth motion. Parts were 3D printed and assembled with precision to maintain alignment and functionality.

During fabrication, considerations included:

  • Material Selection: Durable yet lightweight materials were chosen to minimize wear and ensure reliable motion.
  • Friction Management: Bearings and lubricated joints were incorporated to reduce friction and improve efficiency.
  • Motion Testing: Iterative testing helped refine the gear ratios and linkage configurations for optimal performance.

Future Enhancements

  • Integrating motorized actuation for continuous and automated movements.
  • Exploring the use of flexible materials in linkages to introduce adaptive and responsive movements.
  • Developing larger-scale versions for architectural installations or public kinetic art pieces.
