Pneumatic Silicone Caterpillar Robot

Soft Robotic Caterpillar â€“ Pneumatically Actuated Soft Robot

This project showcases a pneumatically actuated soft robotic caterpillar, designed to emulate biomimetic locomotion inspired by earthworms and caterpillars. The robot combines soft materials with mechanical enhancements to create directional movement using controlled airflow.

Key Features:

Custom Mold Design & Fabrication:

  • Designed and fabricated CAD-based molds for casting the soft silicone body.
  • The mold was created to allow for internal air chambers, enabling expansion and contraction during actuation.

Material Composition:

  • The main body is made from flexible silicone for soft, deformable motion.
  • Integrated metal extensions along the underside, inspired by earthworm setae (bristle-like structures), provide directional friction, allowing forward movement while preventing backward sliding.

Pneumatic Actuation & Control:

  • Controlled via an Arduino microcontroller that manages the airflow.
  • A small air pump is used to inflate and deflate the silicone chambers, simulating "breathing" motions that drive locomotion.
  • Custom Arduino code cycles air pressure to create rhythmic contractions, mimicking the peristaltic movement seen in biological organisms.

Biomimetic Inspiration:

The movement strategy draws from the locomotion of earthworms and caterpillars, using pneumatic actuation in conjunction with the metal bristles to achieve movement across flat surfaces.

Demonstration:

The included video showcases the caterpillar robot in action, highlighting the inflation/deflation cycles and the resulting movement across a flat surface.

Potential Improvements & Future Work:

  • Incorporating multiple air chambers to allow for more complex, segmented movements.
  • Exploring soft sensor integration to provide feedback on deformation and contact with the environment.
  • Refining the actuation timing for smoother and faster locomotion.
