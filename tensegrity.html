Tensegrity Structures – Physical and Simulated

Kinetic Tensegrity Structures – Simulations & Physical Models

Mujoco Simulation

The tensegrity structure was programmatically generated and simulated using the Mujoco physics engine, enabling precise modeling of physical interactions, tension, and compression forces.

The simulated model consists of four interconnected tensegrity subunits, each dynamically moving while maintaining structural integrity. The subunits are designed with mirrored configurations (opposite chirality), introducing symmetry and balance within the dynamic motion.

The simulation showcases how the tensioned cables and compression struts interact, highlighting the unique stability and flexibility inherent in tensegrity structures.

Physical Prototypes

To complement the simulation, physical tensegrity models were constructed, replicating the digital design. The models feature mirrored tensegrity units that demonstrate the same geometric principles explored in the simulation.

Photos include top and side views, offering insights into the geometric relationships between tension and compression elements. The physical models serve as a tangible exploration of tensegrity principles, validating the behavior observed in the Mujoco simulation.

Design & Structural Insights

The project emphasizes the unique balance in tensegrity systems where compression members (struts) float within a network of tensioned cables, creating lightweight yet stable structures.

By combining simulation and physical modeling, the project highlights how tensegrity can be utilized in dynamic architectures, robotics, and kinetic installations.

This dual approach of simulation and real-world modeling demonstrates the potential of tensegrity systems in both structural applications and dynamic, responsive designs.
