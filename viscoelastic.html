Viscoelastic Fluid Simulation

Particle-Based Viscoelastic Fluid Simulation in Julia

This project implements a particle-based viscoelastic fluid simulation in Julia, inspired by the work of Clavet, Beaudoin, and Poulin (2005). The simulation models the complex dynamics of viscoelastic fluids by treating the fluid as a collection of interacting particles, capturing both fluid-like and elastic behaviors.

Key techniques used in the simulation include:

  • Double Density Relaxation: Enforces incompressibility by calculating local densities and applying corrective displacements.
  • Spring-Based Elasticity: Connects particles with springs to simulate elastic responses within the fluid.
  • Spatial Hashing: Implements an efficient neighbor search to ensure scalability and performance.
  • Boundary Handling: Enforces fixed boundary collisions with a sticky response to maintain fluid integrity within the simulation domain.

This approach allows for the realistic simulation of viscoelastic behaviors, balancing fluid flow with elastic deformation. The animated GIF demonstrates these dynamics in action.
